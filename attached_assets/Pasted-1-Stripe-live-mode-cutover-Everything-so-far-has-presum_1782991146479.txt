1. Stripe live-mode cutover. Everything so far has presumably run on TESTING_STRIPE_SECRET_KEY. Going live means: set the live STRIPE_SECRET_KEY, register the production webhook endpoint (https://unihub.live/api/stripe/webhook) in the Stripe live dashboard, and set the live STRIPE_WEBHOOK_SECRET — test and live webhook secrets are different, and a wrong one means every webhook silently fails signature verification (your reconciler would heal it, but you don't want to launch relying on the safety net). Then run one real end-to-end transaction with a real card: signup → verify → trial → subscribe → confirm the webhook flipped you active → cancel via the portal → confirm deactivation. Also decide VAT now: you're in Cyprus selling subscriptions to EU businesses — enable Stripe Tax (or make a deliberate reverse-charge/threshold decision) before the first invoice, not after. Retroactive VAT cleanup is miserable.
2. Replit deployment type vs. your crons. This one is sneaky: your cleanup (2 AM), reconciliation (3 AM), and trial reminders (9 AM) are in-process node-cron jobs. If unihub.live is deployed as a Replit Autoscale deployment, instances scale to zero when idle and your crons simply never fire — no errors, no logs, nothing. You need a Reserved VM (always-on) deployment, or the crons must move to an external scheduler. Check which deployment type you're on today; if it's autoscale, this is a genuine silent failure of three subsystems you just built. While you're in there, confirm all production secrets are set: SESSION_SECRET (the app now correctly refuses to start without it), live Stripe keys, Resend, Turnstile, Apple Wallet PEMs, DATABASE_URL.
3. Email deliverability. Your entire funnel starts with a verification email — if Resend is sending from an unverified domain, those emails land in spam and signup is dead on arrival. Verify your sending domain in Resend (SPF + DKIM DNS records on unihub.live), then test signup with a fresh Gmail and an Outlook address and check where the email lands. Same for password reset and the new trial emails.
Strongly recommended for day one, not strictly blocking:
4. Confirm Neon backups. Check that point-in-time recovery / history retention is enabled on your Neon project. It's a checkbox, and it's the difference between "restore to 5 minutes ago" and "explain to your merchant why their loyalty data is gone."
5. Minimal error visibility + a support channel. You don't need full observability, but right now if the app throws in production you find out when the merchant messages you. Sentry's free tier takes ~30 minutes to wire into both Express and React. And give your merchant one explicit way to reach you (email or WhatsApp) — first-customer support is also your best product research.
6. One small thing I just spotted on your live site: the homepage meta description says "€25/month subscription" — that matches none of your actual prices (€19/€18/€8/€5, bundle €36.99). Stale copy from an earlier iteration. Cosmetic, but a prospective merchant comparing the Google snippet against your pricing page sees an inconsistency, and mismatched price claims are the wrong first impression. Two-minute fix.
7. Full dress rehearsal in production. Before the real merchant, be your own first merchant on unihub.live: fresh signup, verify, configure a loyalty card, scan a stamp with a second phone, add the pass to Apple and Google Wallet (production APNs and wallet certs behave differently than dev), publish a menu, set a shift schedule, subscribe with a real card. Whatever breaks in that hour is exactly what would have broken in front of your customer.
