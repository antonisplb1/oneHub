ROUND 9 — "POWERED BY uniHub.live" ON ALL PUBLIC SURFACES (DESIGN ONLY)

CONTEXT
uniHub adds a small "Powered by uniHub.live" attribution badge to every
customer-facing / public surface. This is purely additive visual branding.
No behavior, data, or logic changes of any kind.

HARD CONSTRAINTS — DO NOT TOUCH
- NO backend logic changes: no routes, controllers, middleware, cron, webhooks
- NO database/schema changes, no migrations, no db:push
- NO changes to QR code GENERATION/encoding logic — the QR content, size,
  error-correction level, and quiet zone must remain byte-identical. The
  badge goes NEXT TO / BELOW the QR in the surrounding layout only, never
  overlapping the QR or its white quiet-zone margin
- NO changes to Stripe, Telegram, wallet pass, or SSE/chat logic
- EXPLICITLY OUT OF SCOPE: Apple/Google Wallet passes (do not touch pass
  templates or pass generation), merchant dashboard internal screens,
  admin screens
- NO new npm dependencies
- NO changes to .env, secrets, or config

THE BADGE — build one shared component
Create client/src/components/PoweredByBadge.tsx:
- Renders: [unihub-mark.svg 20-22px] "Powered by uniHub.live"
- "Powered by" in grey (#999999 on dark, #6E6E6E on light), Poppins Medium,
  ~12-13px; "uniHub" in white (dark bg) or #1A1A1A (light bg), Poppins
  SemiBold; ".live" always #E53935
- Wraps in <a href="https://unihub.live" target="_blank" rel="noopener">
- Prop: variant="dark" | "light" (auto-pick based on the page's background)
- Subtle: opacity ~0.85, small size, centered, never competing with
  merchant content. Use existing /unihub-mark.svg — do not add new assets

WHERE TO ADD IT
First, INVENTORY every public/customer-facing route in the app and list
them in your report. Then add the badge to the footer/bottom of each:
1. Public menu pages (customer-facing menu views)
2. Public shift schedule pages (PublicShifts)
3. Loyalty card customer pages (card view, stamp/redeem screens)
4. Spin wheel customer pages (wheel play + prize screens)
5. Customer-facing auth-adjacent pages if public (e.g., verify-email
   landing), and the public 404 page
6. Any other public surface found in the inventory that I may have
   forgotten — add it there too and flag it in the report
7. QR PRINTABLE/DOWNLOADABLE OUTPUTS: wherever the app renders a QR for
   the merchant to print/download (posters, table cards, download images),
   add the badge into the surrounding printable layout BELOW the QR with
   clear margin. If the printable is generated as an image/canvas, add the
   badge into the canvas layout only if this does not alter the QR pixels;
   otherwise place it in the surrounding HTML/print CSS instead and say so
   in the report

PLACEMENT RULES
- Always bottom of page/layout, centered, with comfortable margin
- On pages with merchant branding (their logo/menu), the badge must be
  visually subordinate — small, muted, last element
- Print stylesheets: ensure the badge prints legibly in black/white
  (the grey text must not disappear — minimum #6E6E6E on white)
